1. Problem Statement

For Kids

  • Children receive harmful content (bullying, predatory, explicit) with zero real-time guardrails.
  • Parents lack visibility without invasive surveillance.
  • No platform offers inbound filtering AND outbound safety in one product.

For Adults

  • Regret-sends cost relationships, jobs, and reputations daily.
  • No tool prevents messages to specific contacts during high-risk states.
  • Public posts lack pre-send tone/compliance review.

For Organizations

  • Executive comms require delegation with audit trails.
  • Compliance needs vetting without reading every message.
  • No platform combines governance + messaging + AI vetting.

Cost of Doing Nothing

ScenarioImpact
Kid exposed to predatory messagingCRITICAL Immeasurable harm
Drunk text to exHIGH Relationship / legal
Executive unvetted public statementHIGH Stock, brand, legal
Employee sends privileged infoCRITICAL Fines, litigation

2. Goals, Non-goals & Success

Goals

  1. Stop bad messages out — tone, content, time, audience, compliance.
  2. Stop bad messages in — quarantine + review + alerts (kids especially).
  3. Unify comms across all channels with consistent enforcement.
  4. Approvals + delegation (AI, human, quorum) with full audit.
  5. First-party chat + voice for consistent enforcement + cost.
  6. COPPA by design.
  7. Cost-optimized AI — deterministic first, LLM second.

Non-goals

  • Reading all iOS SMS system-wide (impossible for 3rd party).
  • Covert monitoring without consent.
  • Deceptive auto-replies.
  • Replacing Slack/Teams — MsgVet governs, not replaces.

7. Kid-First: Family Safety

Build Priority: Milestone A. Everything else depends on proving kid safety. COPPA is non-negotiable.

Inbound Filtering Pipeline

flowchart TD
  MSG[Message Received] --> DET{Deterministic Filters}
  DET -->|Allow list| ALLOW[ALLOW - deliver]
  DET -->|Deny list| BLOCK[HARD BLOCK + quarantine]
  DET -->|Time window fail| Q1[QUARANTINE]
  DET -->|Unknown sender| Q2[QUARANTINE or escalate]
  DET -->|Not resolved| LLM[LLM Classification - cheap model]
  LLM --> CONF{Confidence >= 0.8?}
  CONF -->|No| STRONG[Escalate to strong model]
  CONF -->|Yes| DEC{Decision}
  STRONG --> DEC
  DEC -->|Clean| ALLOW
  DEC -->|Minor concern| FLAG[ALLOW + FLAG for parent]
  DEC -->|HIGH/CRITICAL| Q3[QUARANTINE - parent must release]
  DEC -->|Known threat| BLOCK
  Q3 --> CRISIS{Self-harm or threat?}
  CRISIS -->|Yes| ALERT[CRITICAL parent alert + crisis resources]
  CRISIS -->|No| NOTIFY[Severity-based parent notification]

11. Rule Hierarchy & Policy Engine

Three-tier rule hierarchy: Global rules apply to everyone. Group rules (families, friends, work, custom) override globals. Individual contact rules are the most specific and override everything. Stricter always wins at the same level.

Rule Hierarchy

flowchart TD
  subgraph GLOBAL["GLOBAL RULES (apply to all contacts)"]
    G1["No sends after midnight"]
    G2["All outbound vetted"]
    G3["Profanity filter active"]
  end
  subgraph GROUP["GROUP RULES (override global)"]
    GF["Family: no restrictions on time"]
    GW["Work: professional tone required"]
    GR["Friends: relaxed language OK"]
    GC["Custom groups via aliases"]
  end
  subgraph INDIVIDUAL["INDIVIDUAL RULES (most specific)"]
    I1["Ex: HARD BLOCK always"]
    I2["Boss: don't text after 6PM"]
    I3["Mom: no restrictions"]
    I4["Contractor: biz hours only"]
  end
  GLOBAL --> GROUP
  GROUP --> INDIVIDUAL
  INDIVIDUAL --> RESOLVE{Conflict Resolution}
  RESOLVE --> R1["Most specific wins"]
  RESOLVE --> R2["Stricter wins at same level"]
  RESOLVE --> R3["Explicit override requires admin"]
  RESOLVE --> R4["Every resolution audit-logged"]

15. Smart Scheduling & Delivery

Context-aware delivery. Messages aren't just scheduled by time — they fire based on state, geofence, activity, or combinations. "Send after he lands AND it's after 9AM." "Text when I leave work." "Send 'I'm here' when I arrive."

Smart Schedule Triggers

flowchart TD
  MSG[Queued Message] --> EVAL{Evaluate Triggers}
  EVAL --> TIME[Time Trigger: after 3:30 PM]
  EVAL --> GEO[Geofence Trigger]
  EVAL --> STATE[Recipient State Trigger]
  EVAL --> COMBO[Combination Trigger]
  GEO --> GL[I leave location: work/gym/school]
  GEO --> GA[I arrive at location: home/office/airport]
  GEO --> GR[Recipient arrives/leaves location]
  STATE --> SD[Driving ends]
  STATE --> SF[Flight lands]
  STATE --> SW[Workout finishes]
  STATE --> SM[Meeting ends]
  STATE --> SS[Wakes up / DND off]
  COMBO --> C1["After he lands AND after 9AM"]
  COMBO --> C2["After school AND not driving"]
  COMBO --> C3["When I leave work AND it's not raining"]
  TIME --> VET[Re-vet against current policy]
  GEO --> VET
  STATE --> VET
  COMBO --> VET
  VET --> SEND[Send via channel module]
  SEND --> VERIFY[Delivery verification]

Combination (AND)Multiple triggers"After he lands AND it's after 9 AM," "after school AND not driving"

Delivery Pipeline

flowchart LR
  A[Schedule request] --> B[Validate + idempotency key]
  B --> C[Pin policy snapshot]
  C --> D[Enqueue - Service Bus]
  D --> E[Trigger fires]
  E --> F{Policy changed?}
  F -->|Yes| G[Re-vet]
  F -->|No| H[Channel adapter send]
  G --> H
  H --> I[Capture provider messageId]
  I --> J[Verify delivery receipt]
  J --> K{Delivered?}
  K -->|Yes| L[DELIVERED]
  K -->|No receipt| M[SENT - best effort]
  K -->|Failed| N[Retry 3x backoff]
  N -->|Max retries| O[FAILED - dead letter]
